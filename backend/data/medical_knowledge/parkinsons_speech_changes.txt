Title: Parkinson Speech Changes

Keywords:
speech, voice, jitter, shimmer, hnr, pitch variation, dysphonia

Evidence:
Speech changes in Parkinsonian syndromes may include reduced voice stability, altered pitch variation, increased jitter or shimmer, and reduced harmonic-to-noise ratio. These findings may support monitoring but should not be used alone for diagnosis.

Clinical Use:
This evidence can support interpretation when the speech agent detects borderline, moderate, or high speech-related risk.

Safety:
Speech findings should be interpreted together with clinical examination and neurologist review.
