Title: Parkinson Gait Changes

Keywords:
gait, walking speed, stride variability, freezing, balance, movement instability

Evidence:
Gait-related changes in Parkinsonian disorders may include reduced walking speed, increased stride variability, freezing episodes, and balance instability. These movement findings can support disease severity assessment and follow-up planning.

Clinical Use:
This evidence can support interpretation when the gait agent detects borderline, moderate, or high movement-related risk.

Safety:
Gait findings should be reviewed in clinical context and should not be treated as a final diagnosis.
